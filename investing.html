Investing Philosophy

My interest in financial markets began early, shaped by family influences and experiences during the Global Financial Crisis. I've been personally involved in the stock and options markets, trading my own capital, for roughly 20 years. While I've read widely on investing and trading, I am not a professional money manager or trader. What the process has given me is a fairly honest understanding of myself—my strengths, and more importantly, my weaknesses when it comes to patience, risk tolerance, and conviction. Markets have a way of being very effective mirrors.

BLDR — Builders FirstSource 5.62%

Thesis: The United States has a structural deficit of homes—especially manufactured and modular housing. As building materials and labor costs rise, disciplined operators should be able to compound value by scaling components, systems, and prefabricated execution to meet demand efficiently. Builders FirstSource fits this theme. It is currently my worst-performing position, and I'm okay with that: management has emphasized capital discipline and has repurchased roughly half the share count since 2021. To me, those repurchases are a tangible signal of long-term alignment and a "shrink-the-float" tailwind if the housing deficit persists.
